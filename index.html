☀️ The Sun

Our Radiant Star

The Sun is a 4.6-billion-year-old G-type main-sequence star at the heart of our solar system. Its gravity holds everything together, from the largest planets to the smallest debris.

Diameter1.39M km
Surface Temp5,500°C
CompositionH · He
Age4.6B yrs

Mercury

The Swiftest Planet

Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun. It completes an orbit in just 88 Earth days and has virtually no atmosphere, causing extreme temperature swings.

Diameter4,880 km
Orbit88 days
Moons0
TypeTerrestrial

Venus

Earth's Fiery Twin

Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system, with a thick toxic atmosphere trapping heat in a runaway greenhouse effect. It spins backwards compared to most planets.

Diameter12,104 km
Surface Temp465°C
Moons0
Day Length243 Earth days

🌍 Earth

The Blue Marble · Our Home

Earth is the only known planet to harbor life. Its liquid water, magnetic field, and atmosphere make it uniquely suited to support a rich biosphere.

Mars

The Red Frontier

Mars is a cold desert world with the tallest volcano (Olympus Mons) and deepest canyon (Valles Marineris) in the solar system. It's a prime target for future human exploration.

Diameter6,779 km
Moons2

King of the Planets

Jupiter is the largest planet, with a mass greater than all other planets combined. Its Great Red Spot is a storm that has raged for over 350 years.
